Key Takeaway : The core insight is that **content creation feels harder than approaching people because the cost of failure feels much higher**. In dating, each rejection costs only a few minutes, so it's easy to move on. In content, you might invest hours into a video or post, making poor results feel like a loss of time, energy, and effort.

Both activities have noisy feedback loops—success is influenced by factors outside your control. A great approach can get rejected, and a great piece of content can flop due to timing, algorithms, or randomness. The difference is that content carries a larger perceived sunk cost, which makes failures feel more personal and discouraging.

The solution is to evaluate **process over outcomes**. Instead of judging a post solely by views or engagement, ask:

- Did I create a strong hook?
- Did I tell a compelling story?
- Did I communicate a clear point of view?

If the answer is yes, then the work was successful regardless of immediate results. Long-term creators, traders, researchers, and entrepreneurs survive because they focus on executing a high-quality process consistently rather than becoming emotionally attached to short-term outcomes.

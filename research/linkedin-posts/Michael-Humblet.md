Key takeaways : Enterprise sales isn’t just about closing—it requires a structured system. Many teams fail by investing in large accounts without a clear plan, relying on a single contact, and waiting for opportunities to emerge. Because enterprise cycles can take 12–18 months, this leads to late discovery of what works and wasted time.

A better approach is deliberate and systematic: carefully selecting the right ICP accounts, building multiple relationships within each account (multi-threading across the DMU), and consistently using content and strategic actions to build credibility. Strong enterprise selling also depends on having a clear narrative and repeatable process, not just ad-hoc effort or intuition.

Key Takeaway : The main idea is that B2B sales is entering a **credibility crisis**. Marketing can generate attention, and AI can increase outreach volume, but neither automatically creates trust. Many companies are trying to solve a trust problem with more automation—more emails, DMs, and AI-generated content—when buyers are already skeptical and increasingly resistant to sales interactions.

The opportunity is to **build credibility before the sales conversation starts**. Since most buyers are not actively looking to purchase today, the focus should shift from chasing the small percentage ready to buy now to educating and earning trust with the much larger group that may buy later. This means consistently sharing expertise, insights, proof, and value so prospects already trust you when they eventually enter the market.

**Key takeaway:** In the AI era, attention is abundant but trust is scarce. The companies that win won't necessarily be the ones with the most outreach—they'll be the ones that establish credibility at scale before asking for a meeting or a sale.
